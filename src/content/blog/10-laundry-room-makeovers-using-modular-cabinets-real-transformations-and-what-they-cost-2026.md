## Why Modular Cabinets Are Transforming Laundry Rooms

The laundry room has quietly become one of the most-renovated spaces in American homes. Homeowners are discovering that the same modular cabinet systems designed for garages work exceptionally well in laundry rooms — offering kitchen-grade materials, heavy-duty storage, and fast DIY installation without hiring a contractor. Here are 10 laundry room makeovers that show what is possible with modular cabinets, including configurations, costs, and lessons learned.

## Makeover 1: The Small Stackable Setup (5ft Wall)

**Configuration:** 1 tall utility cabinet + 1 base cabinet with worktop

**Approximate cost:** $800–$1,200

Perfect for apartments and townhomes with side-by-side or stacked washer-dryer units. The tall cabinet stores detergent, cleaning supplies, and linens behind closed doors — no more cluttered shelving. The base cabinet with worktop creates a folding surface right where you need it. KLOVO's 300 lb shelves handle heavy detergent jugs and bulk supplies easily.

## Makeover 2: The Full-Wall Laundry Command Center (8ft Wall)

**Configuration:** 2 wall cabinets + 2 base cabinets + countertop

**Approximate cost:** $1,500–$2,500

This layout maximizes an 8-foot wall opposite or adjacent to the washer and dryer. Upper wall cabinets store rarely-used items and seasonal linens. Base cabinets with a continuous countertop create a generous folding and sorting station. With KLOVO's GlideLock assembly, the entire system goes up in under an hour — no carpenter, no contractor.

## Makeover 3: The Utility Room Overhaul (12ft L-Shape)

**Configuration:** 2 tall cabinets + 3 base cabinets + 2 wall cabinets

**Approximate cost:** $3,000–$4,500

For larger utility rooms that combine laundry with household storage, an L-shaped configuration turns wasted space into a complete home management center. One wall holds laundry-specific storage (detergent, stain removers, iron, drying rack). The perpendicular wall stores household supplies, cleaning equipment, and seasonal items. The modular system means every inch is purposeful.

## Makeover 4: The Narrow Galley Laundry (4ft × 8ft Space)

**Configuration:** 2 tall cabinets flanking washer/dryer + overhead wall cabinets

**Approximate cost:** $1,800–$2,800

Galley-style laundry rooms are common in older homes. Two tall cabinets flanking the washer and dryer use the vertical space that would otherwise go to waste. Overhead wall cabinets bridge the gap above the machines. The result is a narrow room that stores more than most walk-in closets. KLOVO's moisture-resistant TFL finish handles the humidity that laundry rooms generate.

## Makeover 5: The Mudroom-Laundry Combo (10ft Wall)

**Configuration:** 1 tall cabinet + 3 base cabinets + coat hooks + bench area

**Approximate cost:** $2,000–$3,500

Many homes combine the laundry room with a mudroom entry. This makeover dedicates one section to laundry storage and another to entryway organization — coats, shoes, backpacks, and keys. The tall cabinet handles laundry supplies while base cabinets create a bench area for sitting while removing shoes. A single modular system serves both functions seamlessly.

## Makeover 6: The Pet Owner's Laundry Station (6ft Wall)

**Configuration:** 1 base cabinet + 1 tall cabinet + wall-mounted shelf unit

**Approximate cost:** $1,200–$1,800

Pet owners generate extra laundry: pet beds, towels, blankets, and grooming supplies. This setup dedicates one tall cabinet to pet supplies and one base cabinet to a washing prep area with a countertop for folding and sorting. The 300 lb shelves handle bulk dog food bags and heavy pet supply containers that would buckle lighter shelving.

## Makeover 7: The Rental Property Upgrade (6ft Wall)

**Configuration:** 2 base cabinets + 2 wall cabinets

**Approximate cost:** $1,052–$1,600

Landlords upgrading rental properties need durable, attractive storage that tenants will not destroy. KLOVO's TFL finish resists scratches, moisture, and the kind of wear that rental properties absorb. The GlideLock system means a property manager can install cabinets between tenants in under 30 minutes. If the property sells, the cabinets can be disassembled and moved to the next rental.

## Makeover 8: The Luxury Laundry Suite (16ft Double-Wall)

**Configuration:** 4 tall cabinets + 4 base cabinets + 4 wall cabinets + continuous countertop

**Approximate cost:** $5,000–$7,200

For new construction or major renovations, a double-wall laundry suite transforms the room into a functional showpiece. One wall handles laundry operations (sorting, folding, ironing). The opposite wall stores household supplies, cleaning equipment, and overflow pantry items. With KLOVO's kitchen-grade finish, the room looks as polished as the kitchen — because it uses the same caliber of materials.

## Makeover 9: The Basement Laundry Rescue (8ft Wall)

**Configuration:** 2 tall cabinets + 1 base cabinet + worktop

**Approximate cost:** $1,800–$2,800

Basement laundry areas are typically the most neglected storage spaces in the home. This makeover brings order to the chaos with tall cabinets that contain cleaning supplies and laundry essentials, plus a base cabinet that provides a proper folding surface instead of the top of the dryer. KLOVO's moisture-resistant construction is especially important in basements where humidity levels run higher than the rest of the house.

## Makeover 10: The First-Floor Laundry Closet Expansion (3ft + Closet)

**Configuration:** 1 tall cabinet adjacent to closet + interior closet shelf reorganization

**Approximate cost:** $600–$900

Many newer homes hide the laundry in a first-floor closet. Space is extremely limited, but a single tall KLOVO cabinet placed adjacent to the closet opening effectively doubles available storage. The 300 lb shelves store detergent, cleaning supplies, and linens that would otherwise crowd the closet interior. The cabinet's finished appearance means it can face a hallway without looking out of place.
