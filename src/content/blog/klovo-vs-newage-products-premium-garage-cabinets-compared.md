NewAge Products is the brand most premium garage cabinet shoppers compare against everything else. Their Bold and Pro Series have set the bar for what a "showroom garage" looks like, and they've built genuine brand love over the past decade. KLOVO is newer, but we engineered our system to fix the specific frustrations homeowners shared with us about installing and living with NewAge — the time, the hardware count, and the per-foot cost at scale.

This is an honest side-by-side. NewAge makes great cabinets. Here's where the two lines actually differ.

## TL;DR — Quick Verdict

* **Choose NewAge if:** you want welded steel construction, you're committed to a particular Bold or Pro color palette, and you don't mind a multi-hour assembly with a partner.
* **Choose KLOVO if:** you want furniture-grade engineered wood that's quieter and warmer in feel, the fastest assembly available (90 seconds per cabinet), 300 lb shelves as standard, and a Made-in-Georgia, USA build.

## 1\. Assembly: 90 Seconds vs. Several Hours

NewAge Bold 3.0 and Pro 3.0 cabinets ship flat-packed and require traditional assembly — bolts, brackets, doors hung after the fact, and toe-kick alignment. NewAge's own documentation suggests planning a half-day or more for a typical garage configuration, and most homeowners recommend a second person.

KLOVO uses our **patent-pending GlideLock system**: cam locks and alignment dowels pre-installed at the factory. Cabinets snap together in roughly **90 seconds each**, no tools, no partner required. A full 14-foot configuration goes from box to wall-ready in about 30 minutes for one person.

If you've ever spent a Saturday on the garage floor with an Allen wrench and a half-assembled wall cabinet, this difference is hard to overstate.

**Winner: KLOVO** — significantly.

## 2\. Materials: Steel vs. Engineered Wood (TFL)

NewAge Bold and Pro Series use 24-gauge welded steel with a powder-coated finish. It's a proven construction. The steel is durable, the powder coat resists chips, and the look is clean.

KLOVO uses kitchen-grade engineered wood with **TFL (thermally fused laminate)** surfaces and **1mm PVC edge-banding**. These are the same construction standards used in high-end kitchen cabinetry — moisture-resistant, won't rust, won't dent from a dropped wrench, and quieter to open and close.

**Winner: KLOVO.**

Like-for-like — premium configuration with worktop, soft-close hardware, and adjustable shelving — KLOVO is generally **15–25% less per linear foot than NewAge Pro**, and roughly comparable to Bold while including soft-close and 300 lb shelves as standard.
