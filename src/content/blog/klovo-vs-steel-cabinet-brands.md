If you've been shopping for garage cabinets for more than ten minutes, you've already seen the steel brands. Gladiator at Home Depot. Husky on the end cap. NewAge on a few pages of Google results.

They're familiar. They've been in the category for years. And for a lot of buyers, they're the default — not because they're necessarily the best choice, but because they're the most visible one.

This is a straightforward comparison. We're putting Klovo next to the steel brands on the specs that actually matter: weight capacity, depth, finish, assembly experience, and what you get for the price. No spin in either direction.

---

## The brands

**Gladiator** (owned by Whirlpool) is the most widely distributed garage cabinet brand in the US. Available at Home Depot and Lowe's. Steel construction, powder-coated finish. Two lines: the RTA (ready-to-assemble) entry line and the Premier welded line.

**Husky** is Home Depot's house brand. Budget-to-mid price point, steel construction, available in a range of sizes. Very widely sold.

**NewAge Products** positions itself as a premium steel option. Stainless steel or powder-coated steel, sold at Costco and online. Higher price than Gladiator RTA, more style-focused.

**Klovo** is engineered wood with TFL finish, 500 lbs per cabinet, patent-pending GlideLock assembly. Made in USA. Sold through retail partners.

## Weight capacity

This is the number most people look at first, and it's where steel and wood diverge in a meaningful way.

| Brand | Capacity |
|---|---|
| Gladiator RTA | 50–100 lbs per shelf |
| Husky | 52–100 lbs per shelf |
| NewAge | 150–250 lbs per cabinet |
| **Klovo** | **500 lbs per cabinet** |

The key word in those first three is *per shelf*. Steel cabinets typically spec capacity per shelf, which can mislead — a cabinet with three shelves at 100 lbs each is a 300 lb cabinet, but only if the load is perfectly distributed. Klovo specs 500 lbs per cabinet, which is the relevant real-world number.

For light storage — spray cans, cleaning supplies, seasonal bins — any of these options is fine. For a miter saw, a compressor, cases of motor oil, or anything that gets real use, capacity matters.

## Cabinet depth

Depth affects how the cabinet fits in a garage and what it can actually store.

| Brand | Depth |
|---|---|
| Gladiator | 24 inches |
| Husky | 24 inches |
| NewAge | 18–24 inches (varies by line) |
| **Klovo** | **18 inches** |

This sounds counterintuitive — wouldn't deeper be better? In a kitchen, yes. In a garage, 24-inch cabinets eat into your floor space significantly, especially in a single-car or smaller two-car garage. 18-inch depth is the standard for garage-specific cabinetry because it leaves more room to work, park, and move around.

The other issue with 24-inch kitchen-depth cabinets in a garage: they don't actually store garage things better. Sports equipment, tools, and bins don't need 24 inches of depth. They need 18 inches and a door that closes.

## Finish and durability

Steel cabinets are finished with powder coat. Powder coat is durable against impact but vulnerable to two things garages are full of: moisture at seams and edges, and chemicals (cleaners, lubricants, paint thinner). Over time, powder-coated steel can develop rust at seams, corners, and any point where the finish is scratched or chipped.

Klovo uses Thermally Fused Laminate (TFL) — the same finish used in high-end kitchen cabinetry. Scratch-resistant, moisture-resistant, and chemically stable. The exposed edges are sealed with 1mm PVC edge banding. TFL doesn't rust, doesn't chip the way powder coat does, and cleans easily.

The other meaningful difference: push-to-open doors and drawers. Steel cabinets universally use handles and pulls. Klovo's push-to-open means no hardware to snag, no pulls to loosen over time.

## Assembly

**Gladiator RTA:** Cam locks, alignment dowels, Allen wrenches. Per-cabinet assembly time is typically 45–90 minutes for someone who hasn't done it before.

**Husky:** Similar cam-lock assembly. Plan for an hour per cabinet.

**NewAge:** Welded cabinets (their premium line) arrive pre-assembled. The trade-off is shipping — welded steel is heavy and ships LTL freight, which means scheduling a delivery window and typically having two people available.

**Klovo:** GlideLock panels push and lock together. Each cabinet assembles in about 2–3 minutes. A full set in under an hour. Four screws per cabinet secure the back panel runners — that's the only tool required.

Assembly experience is where the difference is most felt. A full 8-foot Klovo set can go together in a Saturday morning. The equivalent Gladiator RTA set is an all-day job.

## The honest summary

Steel cabinets are a reasonable choice for light-use, budget-conscious buyers who don't mind cam-lock assembly and are comfortable with standard powder-coat durability.

For a buyer who wants kitchen-grade finish quality, higher capacity, faster assembly, and garage-specific dimensions — Klovo is the comparison that holds up.
